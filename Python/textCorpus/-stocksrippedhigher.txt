Stocks ripped higher for a second day Tuesday, as a strong batch of corporate earnings assuaged concern over higher rates.The Dow Jones Industrial Average climbed 317 points, or 0.8%. The S&P 500 gained 1.2% while the Nasdaq Composite ticked up 1.6%.Spotify surged nearly 16% after surpassing Wall Street's first-quarter estimates and issuing rosy second-quarter guidance. UPS shares edged 2% higher after the delivery giant posted better-than-expected quarterly earnings. PepsiCo, meanwhile, dipped 2.4% despite reporting better-than-expected earnings. Shares of GE Aerospace added roughly 7% after the company reported an earnings beat.Tesla is slated to report earnings after the bell, followed by Meta Platforms on Wednesday afternoon. Google parent company Alphabet and Microsoft round out the technology-heavy earnings week on Thursday.Roughly 20% of the S&P 500 has reported earnings through Tuesday morning. Of those companies, 76% have beaten analysts' expectations, FactSet data shows.Tuesday's moves come after an upbeat session on Wall Street.
